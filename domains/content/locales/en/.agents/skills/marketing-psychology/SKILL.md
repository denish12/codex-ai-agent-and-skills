# Marketing Psychology

## When to use
- During **strategy development** — choosing positioning, messaging, and funnel structure based on psychology.
- During **brief creation** — deciding which triggers and models to embed in the content.
- During **copywriting** — applying specific persuasion techniques.
- During **CTA optimization** — selecting a psychological lever for conversion.
- During **designing email series** — sequencing triggers across stages.
- During **competitor analysis** — evaluating which psychological tactics they use.

## Inputs

| Field | Required | Description |
|-------|:--------:|-------------|
| Target Audience Persona | ✅ | From `$audience-analysis` — pain points, triggers, barriers, JTBD |
| Business Goal | ✅ | Awareness / leads / conversion / retention |
| Funnel Stage | ✅ | TOFU / MOFU / BOFU — determines the set of models |
| Content Type | ✅ | Post / email / landing page / ad / carousel |
| Product / Offer | ⬚ | What is being promoted — to tie models to specifics |
| Constraints | ⬚ | Legal, ethical, brand — what is not allowed |

> Psychology without persona = blind manipulation. Always start with audience understanding.

## Psychological Models and Triggers

### Models Catalog

#### 1. Reciprocity
**Essence:** When someone receives value for free, they feel obligated to return the favor.
**How to apply:** Provide value BEFORE asking: free guide, tool, checklist, consultation.
**Leverage:** High (TOFU/MOFU)
**Example:** "Download the free 50-point checklist" → then "Try our tool"

#### 2. Social Proof
**Essence:** People follow the actions of others, especially those similar to themselves.
**Types:**
- **Numerical:** "10,000+ followers", "Used by 500+ companies"
- **Authoritative:** "Recommended by [expert]", "As featured in Forbes"
- **Peer:** "Marketers from [your niche] have already switched"
- **Testimonial:** A specific customer quote with a result
- **Case study:** A detailed success story
**Leverage:** Very High (MOFU/BOFU)
**Example:** "847 SaaS companies increased retention by 23% in Q1"

#### 3. Scarcity
**Essence:** Limited availability makes something seem more valuable.
**Types:**
- **By quantity:** "Only 12 spots left"
- **By time:** "Ends Friday", "Last 24 hours"
- **By access:** "Only for the first 100", "Closed access"
**Leverage:** High (BOFU)
**⚠️ Rule:** Scarcity must be **real**. Fake scarcity = losing trust forever.
**Example:** "Enrollment closes on Friday — 7 spots left out of 50"

#### 4. Loss Aversion
**Essence:** The pain of losing is twice as powerful as the pleasure of gaining (Kahneman & Tversky).
**How to apply:** Show what the person loses if they DO NOT act.
**Leverage:** Very High (MOFU/BOFU)
**Example:** "Every day without automation is 3 hours of lost time. That's 60 hours a month."
**⚠️ Balance:** Do not overdo it — excessive fear → rejection. Show the loss → immediately provide the solution.

#### 5. Anchoring
**Essence:** The first number/fact establishes a frame of reference for evaluating everything that follows.
**How to apply:** First state a high price / major problem → then present the alternative.
**Leverage:** High (BOFU, pricing)
**Example:** "An agency charges $5000/mo. Our tool is $99/mo. Same result."

#### 6. Authority
**Essence:** People trust experts, opinion leaders, and institutions.
**How to apply:** Demonstrate expertise: research, expert quotes, certificates, media mentions.
**Leverage:** High (TOFU/MOFU)
**Example:** "According to McKinsey (2026)...", "Recommended by [famous expert]"

#### 7. Commitment & Consistency
**Essence:** People strive to be consistent: if they say "yes" to a small request, they are more likely to say "yes" to a larger one.
**How to apply:** Start with a small request (subscribe) → then a large one (buy). Foot-in-the-door technique.
**Leverage:** Medium-High (entire funnel)
**Example:** "Download free checklist" → "Register for webinar" → "Start trial" → "Subscribe"

#### 8. Curiosity Gap
**Essence:** The brain dislikes incompleteness and seeks to close information gaps.
**How to apply:** Start a story/fact but withhold the conclusion → CTA = learn more.
**Leverage:** Very High (TOFU — hooks, subject lines)
**Example:** "87% of marketers make this mistake. Check — do you?"
**⚠️ Rule:** The curiosity gap must be **closed** within the content. Unclosed gap = clickbait → lost trust.

#### 9. Framing Effect
**Essence:** The same information presented differently elicits different decisions.
**Types:**
- **Positive frame:** "95% of customers are satisfied" (highlights success)
- **Negative frame:** "Stop wasting 5 hours a week on manual work" (highlights loss)
- **Temporal frame:** "Just 15 minutes a day" vs "90 hours a year"
**Leverage:** High (any stage)
**Example:** "Save 30%" (positive) vs "Stop overpaying by 30%" (negative) — the second is stronger.

#### 10. Endowment Effect
**Essence:** People value what they already "have" more than what they do not possess.
**How to apply:** Let them try for free → the person won't want to lose what they're already using.
**Leverage:** High (BOFU — trials, freemium)
**Example:** "14 days free. Set up in 5 minutes" → after trial: "Don't lose your settings and data — upgrade to a paid plan"

#### 11. Paradox of Choice
**Essence:** Too many options → decision paralysis → they choose nothing.
**How to apply:** Limit choices: 2-3 options, one recommended. A single primary CTA.
**Leverage:** High (BOFU — pricing, CTA)
**Example:** Pricing page: 3 plans, middle one = "Most Popular". Not 7 plans.

#### 12. Bandwagon Effect
**Essence:** The more people doing X, the more others want to do X.
**How to apply:** Show momentum: "500 people joined in the last week", "The top trend of 2026".
**Leverage:** Medium-High (TOFU/MOFU)
**Example:** "2000+ marketers joined this month — get on board"

#### 13. Zeigarnik Effect
**Essence:** Unfinished tasks are remembered better than completed ones.
**How to apply:** Open loops in content, series, "part 1 of 5", progress bars.
**Leverage:** Medium (retention, series)
**Example:** "In the next email — the 3rd step that doubled conversion. Don't miss it."

#### 14. IKEA Effect
**Essence:** People value things they helped create more than objective worth.
**How to apply:** Involve them in creation: "Customize your plan", "Build your bundle", calculators, quizzes.
**Leverage:** Medium (MOFU — interactive content)
**Example:** "Take a 2-minute quiz → get a personalized plan"

#### 15. Peak-End Rule
**Essence:** People judge experiences largely based on how they felt at the peak and at the end. The middle is forgotten.
**How to apply:** Insert a WOW moment in the middle of the content + a strong finish (CTA).
**Leverage:** Medium (content structure)
**Example:** Carousel: slide 5 = shocking fact (peak), slide 10 = powerful CTA (end)

## Scoring System — Leverage × Feasibility

### How to select models for a specific task

Evaluate each model on 2 axes (scale 1-5):

| Axis | 1 | 3 | 5 |
|------|---|---|---|
| **Leverage** (impact strength) | Weak influence on audience | Moderate | Direct influence on decision |
| **Feasibility** (ease of execution) | Hard to implement (needs data, budget, time) | Moderate | Easy to embed in content |

**Score = Leverage × Feasibility** (max 25)

### Prioritization Matrix

| Score | Priority | Action |
|:-----:|----------|--------|
| 20-25 | 🟢 **Must Use** | Mandatory to apply |
| 12-19 | 🔵 **Should Use** | Recommended if resources allow |
| 6-11 | 🟡 **Could Use** | Use if convenient |
| 1-5 | ⬚ **Skip** | Not worth the effort |

## Model Combinations by Tasks

### Landing Page (conversion)
**Recommended stack:** Authority + Social Proof + Loss Aversion + Scarcity + Anchoring
```
[Authority] "According to McKinsey..."
[Loss Aversion] "You're losing 60 hours a month on manual work"
[Social Proof] "500+ companies have already automated"
[Anchoring] "Agency = $5000/mo. Us = $99/mo"
[Scarcity] "Price increases in 3 days"
```

### Email Series (nurturing)
**Recommended stack:** Reciprocity → Commitment → Social Proof → Loss Aversion → Scarcity
```
Email 1: [Reciprocity] Free guide
Email 2: [Commitment] Quiz / checklist (small action)
Email 3: [Social Proof] Client case study
Email 4: [Loss Aversion] What you lose without the solution
Email 5: [Scarcity] Offer with a deadline
```

### Social Media Post (awareness)
**Recommended stack:** Curiosity Gap + Authority + Bandwagon
```
[Curiosity Gap] Hook: "87% of marketers make this mistake"
[Authority] Data from a study
[Bandwagon] "Join 10K+ followers"
```

### Carousel (education + engagement)
**Recommended stack:** Curiosity Gap + Peak-End + Zeigarnik + Commitment
```
Slide 1: [Curiosity Gap] Hook headline with intrigue
Slide 2-4: [Commitment] Content (micro-yesses: "Did you know? → No? → Here it is:")
Slide 5: [Peak-End] Peak insight / shocking fact
Slide 9-10: [Peak-End] Strong finish + CTA
[Zeigarnik] "Part 2 — tomorrow" (if part of a series)
```

### Pricing Page
**Recommended stack:** Anchoring + Paradox of Choice + Social Proof + Endowment
```
[Anchoring] Show the most expensive plan first
[Paradox of Choice] 3 plans (not 7), middle = "Most Popular"
[Social Proof] "The choice of 70% of clients"
[Endowment] "14 days free — start right now"
```

## Protocol

### Step 1 — Context
1. Identify the persona, business goal, funnel stage, and content type.
2. Identify constraints (ethical, legal, brand).

### Step 2 — Model Selection
1. Using the funnel scoring table — select **3-5 models** with the highest leverage for the stage.
2. For each — evaluate **feasibility** (do you have data for social proof? is the scarcity real?).
3. Calculate **Score = Leverage × Feasibility**.
4. Sort them. Select the **top-3** (Must Use + Should Use).

### Step 3 — Application
1. Determine **where in the content** to apply each model (hook / body / CTA / P.S.).
2. For each — draft **specific wording** (not abstract concepts, but actual text).
3. Verify **ethics** (see below).

### Step 4 — Combination
1. Ensure models **do not conflict** (Scarcity + "we're for everyone" = conflict).
2. Ensure there are **no more than 3-4** models per single content unit (overload → manipulative).
3. Structure the **sequence**: from soft (Curiosity, Authority) to hard (Scarcity, Loss Aversion).

### Step 5 — Ethical Check
Go through the checklist:
- [ ] Scarcity is real (not a fake deadline)?
- [ ] Social proof is truthful (no fabricated numbers)?
- [ ] Loss aversion is not excessive (shows loss, but doesn't resort to fear-mongering)?
- [ ] Curiosity gap is closed within the content (no clickbait)?
- [ ] The person **will not regret** their decision afterward (the value is real)?

> **Rule:** If a model only works through deception — do not use it. Ethical psychology = demonstrating **real** value more effectively.

## Ethical Boundaries

| ✅ Ethical | ❌ Unethical |
|-----------|------------|
| Real scarcity (spots are actually limited) | Fake countdown timer resetting automatically |
| Social proof with real figures | Fabricated reviews and numbers |
| Loss aversion: showing the real cost of inaction | Fear-mongering: intimidation without a solution |
| Curiosity gap closed within the text | Clickbait: hook with zero substance |
| Anchoring using actual competitor prices | Inflating "original" prices to hallucinate a discount |
| Reciprocity: providing value for free | Guilt-tripping: "we gave, now you owe us" |
| Framing: deciding on the presentation angle | Distorting data via cherry-picking |

---

## Validation (Quality Gate)

Application of psychology is considered ready if:

- [ ] Persona is defined (models are tied to specific pain points/triggers)
- [ ] 2-4 models selected (no more — prevent overload)
- [ ] For each model: Score (Leverage × Feasibility) is calculated
- [ ] For each model: specific wording is created (no abstractions)
- [ ] Models do not conflict with each other
- [ ] Sequence flows reasonably: soft → hard
- [ ] Ethical check passed (5 items)
- [ ] Curiosity gaps are closed within the content
- [ ] Scarcity is real (if used)

## Anti-patterns

| Mistake | Why it's bad | How to do it right |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| 7+ models in one post | Manipulative overload, leads to rejection | 2-4 models maximum |
| Fake scarcity | Permanent loss of trust | Only use real limitations |
| Curiosity without closure | Clickbait → unfollowing | Always close the gap in the content |
| Only negative framing | Fear → rejection | Balance: expose the loss → provide the solution |
| Psychology without persona | Tactics miss the mark | Tie models directly to specific audience pains |
| Loss aversion for Tier A | VIPs don't need to be intimidated | Tier A = Exclusivity and reciprocity |
| Scarcity for TOFU | Cold traffic won't buy under pressure | Scarcity = BOFU. TOFU = Curiosity, Authority |
| Copying without adaptation | "Only 3 spots left" for SaaS with unlimited tier | Ensure scarcity remains plausible |
