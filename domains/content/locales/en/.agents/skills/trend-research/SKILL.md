# Trend Research

## When to Use
- When **planning content** — integrating trends into the `$content-calendar`.
- When **creating situational content** — a trend happening right now that needs a fast reaction.
- For **regular monitoring** — weekly scans of the trend landscape.
- When **expanding to a new platform** — understanding what works over there right now.

## Input

| Field | Required | Description |
|-------|:--------:|-------------|
| Niche / Theme | ✅ | The industry area to monitor for trends |
| Platforms | ✅ | From `$platform-strategy` — where to monitor |
| Audience Persona | ✅ | From `$audience-analysis` — what resonates with the audience |
| Brand Guidelines | ✅ | From `$brand-guidelines` — for assessing brand fit |
| Period | ⬚ | 7 / 14 / 30 days (default 14) |
| Competitors | ⬚ | From `$competitor-content-analysis` — which trends competitors are using |

> If the niche is missing — **request it**. A "general" trend doesn't exist — trends are niche-specific.

## Trend Types

| Type | Description | Example | Lifespan |
|------|-------------|---------|----------|
| **Audio / Sound** | Viral audio, music, meme sound | Trending sound in TikTok/Reels | 3-14 days |
| **Format** | New content type / delivery style| Photo dumps, "POV:", text-on-screen templates | 2-8 weeks |
| **Thematic** | Hot topic, discussion, news | AI hype, "quiet quitting", market crash | 1-4 weeks |
| **Visual** | Style, filter, aesthetic | AI portraits, film grain, Y2K aesthetic | 4-12 weeks |
| **Challenge** | Challenges, marathons | Ice bucket, flip the switch, transition trends | 1-3 weeks |
| **Meme** | Viral meme template / format | Distracted boyfriend, "Nobody: ...", Drake | 3-14 days |
| **Seasonal** | Tied to time of year, holidays | Back to school, Black Friday, Christmas | Predictable, annual |

## Trend Lifecycle

```
Popularity
     │
     │              ╱ Mainstream
     │             ╱  (everyone uses it)
     │            ╱
     │           ╱ Growth
     │          ╱  (picking up)
     │         ╱
     │        ╱
     │  Early╱                    ╲ Decline
     │      ╱                      ╲ (fading)
     │     ╱                        ╲
     │    ╱                          ╲ Dead
     └──╱────────────────────────────────── Time
     Emerging   Rising   Peak   Falling   Over
```

| Phase | Description | Brand Action | Reaction Speed |
|-------|-------------|--------------|----------------|
| **Emerging** | Seen with innovators, no mass adoption yet | Ideal: be early, high engagement | 24-48 hours |
| **Rising** | Growing, early adopters are using it | Good: not oversaturated yet | 3-7 days |
| **Peak** | Mainstream, everyone is doing it | Okay: if you have a unique angle. No angle = noise | 1-3 days (or skip) |
| **Falling** | Peak has passed, engagement dropping | Not recommended: looks outdated | — |
| **Over** | Dead trend | Forbidden: reputational risk | — |

> **Rule:** It is better to skip a trend than to be late. Emerging/Rising = green zone. Peak = with caution. Falling = no.

## Brand Fit — does the trend match the brand?

Before using a trend — verify it against 5 criteria:

| Criterion | Question | 🟢 Fits | 🔴 Doesn't fit |
|-----------|----------|---------|----------------|
| **Values** | Is the trend compatible with brand values? | Productivity trend → Efficiency brand | "Quiet quitting" trend → HR platform brand |
| **Audience** | Does our persona engage in this trend? | GenZ + TikTok trend → Our persona is GenZ | B2B CEO + TikTok dance → Mismatch |
| **Platform**| Is the trend on our priority platform? | Reels trend → We are active on Instagram | TikTok trend → We don't have TikTok |
| **Tone** | Is the trend compatible with brand ToV? | Humorous trend → Brand has playful tone | Meme trend → Serious B2B brand |
| **Adaptability**| Can the product/topic be organically integrated? | Organizing trend → Our product is a planner | Food trend → We sell SaaS |

**Scoring:** 5/5 🟢 = mandatory. 4/5 = yes, with adaptation. 3/5 = proceed with caution. ≤ 2/5 = skip.

## Protocol

### Step 1 — Preparation
1. Define niche, platforms, persona.
2. Determine the monitoring period (7/14/30 days).
3. Select the monitoring tools per platform.

### Step 2 — Trend Gathering
1. For each platform — check trending sections, hashtags, Explore/For You.
2. For cross-platform tools — review Google Trends, BuzzSumo, Exploding Topics.
3. Check what competitors are using.
4. Record each trend: name, type, platform, lifecycle phase.

### Step 3 — Classification
For each trend:
1. Identify the **type** (audio, format, thematic, visual, challenge, meme, seasonal).
2. Determine the **phase** (Emerging → Rising → Peak → Falling → Over).
3. Evaluate the **metrics** (views, engagement, frequency of use).

### Step 4 — Brand Fit
For trends in Emerging / Rising / Peak phases:
1. Run through the **5 brand fit criteria**.
2. Calculate the score.
3. Filter out trends ≤ 2/5.

### Step 5 — Reaction Speed
For trends that passed the brand fit:
1. Determine the **window of opportunity** — time available for adaptation.
2. Prioritize: 24-48 hours = urgent, 3-7 days = planned, 1+ week = to calendar.

### Step 6 — Adaptation
For each recommended trend — create a specific adaptation plan:
1. **Format** — how to present it (reel, carousel, post, thread).
2. **Angle** — how to tie the trend to our topic/product.
3. **Hook** — how to start (trend = attention, brand = value).
4. **CTA** — where to direct attention.

### Step 7 — Seasonal and Planned
1. Record **upcoming seasonal trends** (holidays, events).
2. Add them to `$content-calendar` in advance (2-4 weeks ahead).

## Validation (Quality Gate)

Trend research is complete if:

- [ ] Platforms are monitored (trending sections, tools)
- [ ] Trends are classified by type and lifecycle phase
- [ ] Brand fit is assessed for each trend (5 criteria)
- [ ] Trends with a score ≤ 2/5 are discarded
- [ ] Reaction speed is defined (urgent / planned / to calendar)
- [ ] A specific adaptation plan (format, angle, hook) exists for recommendations
- [ ] Fading trends are marked (do not use)
- [ ] Seasonal trends are logged for the calendar

## Anti-patterns

| Mistake | Why it's bad | How to do it right |
|---------|--------------|--------------------|
| Being late to a trend | Falling/Over trend = looking outdated | Monitor regularly, react to Emerging/Rising |
| Trend for trend's sake | Brand disconnected from trend — audience is confused | Brand fit ≥ 3/5, organic connection |
| Serious brand + meme trend| Tone mismatch → cringe | Ensure Tone fit via brand fit criteria |
| Only one type of trend | e.g. all reels look identical | Combine types: audio, format, thematic |
| No adaptation (copying) | "Just like everyone else" — no unique brand value | Trend = format, brand = content & angle |
| Ignoring seasonal trends | Everyone wishes a Happy New Year, while the brand is silent | Plan seasonal content 2-4 weeks ahead |
| Monitoring once a month | Trends live for 3-14 days — monthly is too slow | Monitor at least once a week |
| Using controversial trends| Reputational risk | Brand fit: values + tone. When in doubt, skip |
