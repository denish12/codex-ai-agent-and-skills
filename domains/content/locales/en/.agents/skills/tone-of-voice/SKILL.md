# Tone of Voice

## When to Use
- When **creating a ToV from scratch** — for a new brand or rebranding.
- When **writing any text** — the copywriter cross-references the ToV document.
- During **copywriter onboarding** — to calibrate their writing style.
- When **launching on a new platform** — to adapt the tone.
- As an **expanded supplement** to the ToV section within `$brand-guidelines`.

## Input

| Field | Required | Description |
|-------|:--------:|-------------|
| Brand Identity | ✅ | From `$brand-guidelines` — mission, values, archetype, positioning |
| Audience Personas | ✅ | From `$audience-analysis` — to adapt tone across different segments |
| Platforms | ✅ | From `$platform-strategy` — for platform-specific adaptation |
| Existing Content | ⬚ | Brand text samples (if available) — to analyze the current tone |
| Competitors | ⬚ | From `$competitor-content-analysis` — to differentiate the tone |

> If Brand Identity is not provided — **request it** or redirect to `$brand-guidelines`. A ToV without a brand core equates to a random style.

## Link to Brand Identity

ToV is the **vocal expression** of the Brand Identity:

| Brand Identity | → | Tone of Voice |
|----------------|---|---------------|
| Mission | → | What we talk about (topics, focus) |
| Values | → | How we talk (communication principles) |
| Archetype | → | Character of the voice (friendly Caregiver vs provocative Rebel) |
| Positioning | → | Level of expertise and confidence |

> **Rule:** ToV cannot contradict the Brand Identity. If the brand archetype is "Sage", the tone cannot be "meme-heavy and provocative".

## Protocol

### Step 1 — Voice Characteristics
1. Define **4-6 adjectives** that describe the brand's voice.
2. For each, provide a 1-sentence explanation of what it means in the context of the brand.
3. Link it to the Brand Identity: what value / archetype element drives each characteristic.

### Step 2 — Tone Axes (7 Axes)
Position the brand on each axis (from 1 to 10):

| Axis | 1 | 10 | What it dictates |
|------|---|----|------------------|
| **Formality** | Formal, business-like | Informal, conversational | Register of language |
| **Seriousness** | Serious, restrained | Playful, funny | Acceptability of humor |
| **Emotionality** | Reserved, factual | Emotional, expressive | Level of emotion in the text |
| **Expertise** | Accessible, simple | Expert, deep | Depth and terminology |
| **Pacing** | Measured, thorough | Fast, energetic | Text rhythm, sentence length |
| **Distance** | Detached, neutral | Close, personal | "We and you" vs "Hey friend" |
| **Boldness** | Cautious, consensus | Provocative, opinionated | Readiness to take a stance |

### Step 3 — Speech Patterns
Establish specific rules:

| Element | Options | Brand's Choice |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| **Pronouns** | You (informal) / You (formal) / We / Impersonal | [choice + rationale] |
| **Emojis** | Never / Rarely (1-2) / Actively | [rule + examples] |
| **Slang** | Banned / Industry-specific allowed / Youth slang allowed | [rule] |
| **Sentence Length** | Short (5-10 words) / Medium (10-20) / Long (20+) | [preference] |
| **Questions to reader**| Never use / Sometimes / Actively | [rule] |
| **Exclamation marks** | Never use / Rarely / Acceptable | [rule] |
| **Numbers** | Spelled out / Digits / Context-dependent | [rule] |
| **Abbreviations** | Always expand / At first mention / Allowed unexpanded | [rule] |
| **Foreign words** | Translate / Transliterate / Keep original | [industry rule] |

### Step 4 — "We Say / We Don't Say" by Categories
For each category — provide at least 3 examples:

**Categories:**
1. **Greeting / addressing** — how we start a conversation.
2. **Product description** — how we talk about ourselves.
3. **Pain point description** — how we describe audience struggles.
4. **CTA** — how we call users to action.
5. **Reacting to success** — how we talk about wins.
6. **Reacting to issues** — how we handle mistakes or crises.
7. **Expertise** — how we showcase our knowledge.

### Step 5 — Stop-List
Banned words and phrasing structures:

| Category | Banned | Why | Replacement |
|----------|--------|-----|-------------|
| Bureaucratese | "Implement", "in the framework of", "the given" | Dead language | "Do", "in", "this" |
| Corporate cliches | "Innovative", "synergy", "ecosystem" | Empty words | Specifics: what exactly is innovative |
| Manipulation | "Don't miss your chance!!!" | Pressure, breeds distrust | "Try it — you'll like it" |
| [Brand-specific] | [words that don't fit the brand] | [rationale] | [replacements] |

### Step 6 — Persona Adaptation
If the target audience has multiple segments, the tone may adapt:

| Persona | Tone Adaptation | Example |
|---------|-----------------|---------|
| [Persona 1 — beginners] | Simpler, warmer, jargon-free | "Let's figure out together how this works" |
| [Persona 2 — experts] | Deeper, more specific, jargon allowed | "Reduced CAC by 30% via cohort retargeting" |
| [Persona 3 — CEO] | More confident, data-driven, no fluff | "340% ROI in Q1. Here is how we did it" |

> **Rule:** Adapt the **intensity** of the tone, not the core tone itself. The brand must remain recognizable.

### Step 7 — Platform Adaptation

| Platform | Formality (shift from base) | Emojis | Length | Specifics |
|----------|-----------------------------|--------|--------|-----------|
| **LinkedIn** | +1-2 towards formal | Minimum (0-1) | Med-Long | Expert, data, insights |
| **Instagram** | -1-2 towards informal | Allowed (1-3) | Short | Visual, inspiring, strong hooks |
| **TikTok** | -2-3 towards informal | Allowed | Very Short| Conversational, authentic, trends |
| **Telegram** | Base | Minimum | Medium | Informative, direct, formatted |
| **Email** | +1 towards formal | Contextual | Medium | Personal, warm, clear CTA |
| **YouTube** | -1 towards informal | In desc. | Conversational | As if speaking to a friend |
| **Blog** | Base | None | Long | Expert, but not academic |

> Shifts should be ±1-3 from the base position on the axes. It is not a complete change of tone.

### Step 8 — Situational Adaptation

| Situation | How Tone Changes | Example |
|-----------|------------------|---------|
| **Good News** | +Emotionality, +Energy | "We did it! 10,000 users in the first month 🎉" |
| **Educational** | +Expertise, -Emotionality | "Here are 5 proven steps to..." |
| **Sales/Promo** | +Confidence, +Value | "Try it for free — and see why 500+ companies chose us" |
| **Crisis / Mistake**| +Seriousness, +Accountability, -Humor | "We made a mistake. Here is what happened and how we are fixing it" |
| **Support / Help** | +Warmth, +Closer Distance | "We are here for you. Message us and we will sort it out together" |
| **Behind the Scenes**| +Informality, +Closeness | "Here is what our office looks like on a Friday evening" |
| **Holidays** | +Emotionality, +Warmth | "Happy New Year! Thank you for being with us" |

> **Rule:** The situation shifts the axes but does not break the brand. Even in a crisis, the brand still sounds like itself.

## Validation (Quality Gate)

A ToV is considered complete if:

- [ ] Voice characteristics are defined (4-6 adjectives with explanations)
- [ ] All 7 axes are mapped with the brand's position (1-10)
- [ ] Speech patterns are formalized (pronouns, emojis, slang, length, questions)
- [ ] "We Say / We Don't Say" includes at least 3 examples across 7 categories
- [ ] Stop-List is compiled (with replacements)
- [ ] Persona adaptation is defined (if target audience is heterogeneous)
- [ ] Platform adaptation is outlined (for all priority platforms)
- [ ] Situational adaptation includes at least 5 scenarios
- [ ] The ToV does not contradict `$brand-guidelines` (mission, values, archetype)
- [ ] Approved by the user

## Anti-patterns

| Mistake | Why it's bad | How to do it right |
|---------|--------------|--------------------|
| ToV = 2 adjectives | "Friendly and professional" applies to 90% of brands | 4-6 characteristics + axes + patterns + examples |
| ToV without examples | Abstract rules mean everyone interprets them differently | Minimum of 3 "we say / we don't say" examples per category |
| Same tone everywhere | Using a LinkedIn tone on TikTok feels unnatural | Adapt based on the platform (shift the axes) |
| ToV disconnected from brand | The tone doesn't align with mission and values | ToV = vocal expression of the Brand Identity |
| ToV without a stop-list | The copywriter relies on cliches | Explicit stop-list with suggested replacements |
| One tone for all personas | A CEO and a Gen-Z intern require different communication | Adapt the tone's intensity based on the persona |
| Humor during a crisis | The audience perceives it as disrespectful | Situational adaptation: crisis = seriousness |
| ToV = unread document | No practical application | Create quick reference cards for copywriters: "Our tone is... We say... We don't say..." |
